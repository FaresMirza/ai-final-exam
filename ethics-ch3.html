Chapter 3

Software Safety: Liability and Practice

Why Software Safety Matters

  • Software now controls aircraft, medical devices, industrial processes, and other high-risk systems.
  • If software fails in these areas, the result can be injury, death, or environmental damage.
  • That is why safety questions are not “extra quality issues”; they are core engineering obligations.

What Software Safety Means

  • Software safety means preventing harm to people, property, and the environment.
  • It is broader than “the program works” because a correct-looking system can still be unsafe.
  • The goal is to fail safely, not dangerously, so that faults do not turn into disasters.

Safety-Critical Systems and Liability

  • Safety-critical systems are systems whose failure can cause injury, death, or major damage.
  • Examples include air traffic control, medical treatment systems, transport control, and nuclear systems.
  • When software fails, an exam question often becomes: who is legally and ethically responsible?

Liability Concepts

Fault-based ideas

  • Negligence: failure to use reasonable care
  • Product liability: harm caused by a defective product
  • Breach of contract/warranty: failure to meet promised standards

Broader responsibility

  • Strict liability: responsibility even without proving negligence
  • Vicarious liability: employer responsibility for employees
  • Personal liability: an individual is directly accountable for their own actions

These ideas explain whether responsibility comes from carelessness, defective software, broken contractual promises, or organizational responsibility.

Risk Mitigation and Professional Duty

  • Reduce risk through hazard analysis, strong testing, redundancy, fault tolerance, and clear safety documentation.
  • Engineers must report risks and refuse unsafe shortcuts, not merely satisfy the minimum legal standard.
  • Real software failures show that poor design assumptions and weak safeguards can lead to serious harm.
Takeaway

In safety-critical software, ethical responsibility and legal liability are tightly connected.
