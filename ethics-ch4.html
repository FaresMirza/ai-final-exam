Chapter 4

Reading and Discussing a News Article

Start with the Real Question: Who Is Affected?

  • When reading a technology story, first identify the people and groups affected by it.
  • Think about users, non-users, companies, governments, workers, and vulnerable groups.
  • The point is not only to summarize the article, but to ask what the software decision means for real people.

Then Ask: What Is the Impact?

  • Consider both the benefits and the harms created by the system or event.
  • Look beyond the immediate user and think about social, economic, and even environmental effects.
  • An exam-style answer should mention both direct impact and wider long-term consequences.

Identify the Ethical Principles Involved

  • Typical principles include privacy, transparency, accountability, fairness, safety, and honesty.
  • Use the ACM Code of Ethics to decide which principles are being respected or violated.
  • A good answer says not only “this is unethical,” but which ethical principle is at stake.

Professional Responsibility: What Should the Engineer Do?

  • Ask what the responsible engineer should do when they notice a problem.
  • Business pressure does not cancel duties around safety, truthfulness, fairness, or privacy.
  • Professional responsibility may mean documenting concerns, escalating risk, or refusing unsafe work.
Practical frame

Don’t stop at “is this good or bad?” Push into “what should the engineer do next?”

Misuse, Bias, and Regulation

  • Ask whether the technology can be misused, abused, or applied unfairly.
  • Look for bias, discrimination, unequal access, and hidden design assumptions.
  • Also ask whether law, standards, or professional oversight are needed to control the risk.
