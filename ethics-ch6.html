Computer Contracts

What a Computer Contract Is

A computer contract is a legal agreement related to software, computer systems, or IT services.

  • It may cover development, licensing, maintenance, or cloud services.
  • It defines expectations, duties, and legal obligations.
  • In software work, contracts are part of responsible engineering, not just legal paperwork.

Why Contracts Matter

  • They define responsibilities and reduce disputes.
  • They help control scope, schedule, and payment.
  • They provide legal protection for both sides.
  • Common forms include development contracts, licenses, SLAs, outsourcing deals, and agile contracts.

Important Contract Clauses

Project control

  • Payment terms
  • Scope of work
  • Termination
  • Dispute resolution

Trust and risk

  • Confidentiality / NDA
  • Intellectual property
  • Liability
  • Warranties

Ethics Inside Contract Clauses

  • Engineers should use fair terms, honest wording, and transparent expectations.
  • NDAs can protect legitimate secrets, but they can also be abused to hide wrongdoing.
  • The Frances Haugen example shows how legal duty and public-interest ethics can conflict.
  • Liability clauses should not become unethical disclaimers that avoid all responsibility.

Scope Creep and Best Practice

  • Scope creep means extra work outside the agreed contract.
  • Changes should be clarified and documented instead of exploited.
  • Use plain language, involve both legal and technical teams, and include fair dispute processes.
  • Software engineers should understand every contract they sign and speak up against unfair terms.
Exam takeaway

Good contracts are not only enforceable; they are also fair, clear, and consistent with professional integrity.
